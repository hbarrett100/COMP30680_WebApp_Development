What is yoga?

Yoga originated in India and is a physical, mental and spiritual practise. The ultimate goal of yoga is to attain harmony in the mind and spirit by means of poses, breath control, relaxation and meditation. A lot of people who are familiar with yoga poses aren't aware of the many health benefits involved.

Stress reduction

Yoga combines many stress-relieving techniques including exercise and learning to control the breath, clear the mind and relax the body. Developing a stronger connection between our mind and body helps us to deal with stressful situations. What's more, yoga impacts upon the parasympathetic nervous system and with regular practise can decrease the secretion of cortisol, the primary stress hormone.

Increased muscle tone

Balancing in yoga poses requires control of all of your muscles. In particular, yoga targets and strengthens the muscles of the core, arms, legs and back. Holding yoga poses for extended periods of time builds strength and anaerobic endurance of muscles.

Sense of well-being

Yoga practise involves being mindful which helps to boost our compassion and gratitude towards others and ourselves. Yoga also can help improve your sleep, which in turn gives you more energy and focus during the day. More energy and focus during the day can result in better food choices and more physical activity. The list is endless!
